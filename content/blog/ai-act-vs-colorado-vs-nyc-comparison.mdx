AI regulation is no longer just a European concern. If you're a SaaS company selling into multiple markets, you may need to comply with the EU AI Act, Colorado's AI Act, and New York City's Local Law 144 (LL144)—or all three. Each regulation has different scope, requirements, and deadlines. This guide breaks down the key differences so you can prioritize your compliance efforts.

## Geographic Scope: Where Do These Laws Apply?

### EU AI Act
- **Applies to**: Any company placing AI systems on the EU market or whose AI systems affect people in the EU
- **Coverage**: 27 EU member states (440+ million people)
- **Extraterritorial reach**: YES—applies to non-EU companies

### Colorado AI Act
- **Applies to**: Companies doing business in Colorado that deploy "high-risk AI systems"
- **Coverage**: Colorado only (~5.8 million people)
- **Extraterritorial reach**: YES—if you have Colorado customers

### NYC Local Law 144 (LL144)
- **Applies to**: Employers using AI for employment decisions in NYC
- **Coverage**: New York City only (~8.3 million people)
- **Extraterritorial reach**: Limited—applies to employers with NYC employees or candidates

## What Counts as "High-Risk AI"?

### EU AI Act (Annex III)
High-risk AI includes 8 categories:
1. Biometrics
2. Critical infrastructure
3. Education
4. **Employment** (recruiting, performance management)
5. Essential services (credit, healthcare)
6. Law enforcement
7. Migration/border control
8. Administration of justice

**Key point:** Very broad—covers most AI-driven decisions about people.

### Colorado AI Act
"High-risk AI" means AI that makes or substantially assists **consequential decisions**:
- Employment (hiring, firing, promotions)
- Education (admissions, grading)
- Financial services (credit, insurance)
- Government services (benefits, licensing)
- Healthcare
- Housing
- Legal services

**Key point:** Narrower than EU—focused on "consequential decisions."

### NYC LL144
Applies specifically to **automated employment decision tools (AEDTs)**:
- AI used to screen candidates
- AI used to evaluate employees for promotion/termination
- AI that substantially assists hiring/promotion decisions

**Key point:** Narrowest scope—only employment AI in NYC.

## Key Requirements Comparison

| Requirement | EU AI Act | Colorado AI Act | NYC LL144 |
|------------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| **Risk Assessment** | Mandatory (Article 9) | Mandatory | Not required |
| **Bias Audit** | Implied (fairness testing) | Mandatory (annually) | **Mandatory (annually)** |
| **Technical Documentation** | Mandatory (Annex IV) | Risk assessment summary | Not required |
| **Human Oversight** | Mandatory (Article 14) | Not specified | Not specified |
| **Transparency Notice** | Mandatory (Article 50) | Mandatory | **Mandatory (notice to candidates/employees)** |
| **Data Governance** | Mandatory (Article 10) | Not specified | Not required |
| **Post-Market Monitoring** | Mandatory (Article 72) | Impact assessments | Not required |

## Deadlines

- **EU AI Act**: August 2, 2026 (high-risk systems)
- **Colorado AI Act**: February 1, 2026
- **NYC LL144**: Already in effect since July 5, 2023

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Regulation | Maximum Fine |
|-----------|-------------|
| **EU AI Act** | €15M or 3% of global revenue |
| **Colorado AI Act** | $500k per violation (if algorithmic discrimination found) |
| **NYC LL144** | Up to $1,500 per violation (can add up quickly) |

## Multi-Jurisdiction Compliance Strategy

If you sell into multiple markets:

1. **Start with the strictest regulation**: The EU AI Act has the broadest requirements. If you're EU AI Act compliant, you're 80% of the way to Colorado/NYC compliance.

2. **Focus on employment AI first**: Employment AI is covered by all three regulations. If you use AI for hiring, prioritize this use case.

3. **Implement bias testing**: Both Colorado and NYC require annual bias audits. Build this into your process early.

4. **Document everything**: Even if a regulation doesn't require documentation, having it makes compliance easier across jurisdictions.
