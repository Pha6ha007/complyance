Annex III of the EU AI Act is the definitive list of high-risk AI applications. If your SaaS product falls into any of these eight categories, you're subject to the full weight of EU AI Act compliance obligations. This guide breaks down each category with practical examples so you can determine if your product is affected.

## Category 1: Biometrics

AI systems used for biometric identification, categorization, or emotion recognition. This includes:
- Facial recognition for authentication
- Voice biometrics for identity verification
- Emotion detection in customer service
- Biometric categorization (inferring race, gender, etc.)

**SaaS Example:** Video conferencing tools with emotion detection, identity verification services, or attendance tracking systems using facial recognition.

## Category 2: Critical Infrastructure

AI systems used as safety components in critical infrastructure management:
- Water supply management
- Gas/electricity distribution
- Transportation control systems
- Internet infrastructure

**SaaS Example:** Rare for typical SaaS—applies to IoT platforms managing smart city infrastructure or utilities.

## Category 3: Education & Vocational Training

AI systems that:
- Determine access to educational institutions
- Evaluate learning outcomes
- Assess students for grouping or tracking
- Detect exam cheating

**SaaS Example:** Learning management systems (LMS) that use AI to grade, recommend courses, or flag plagiarism; student evaluation tools.

## Category 4: Employment, Workers Management, and Access to Self-Employment

This is the **most common category** for SaaS products. Includes AI used for:
- Recruiting and candidate screening
- Hiring decisions
- Promotion and termination decisions
- Task allocation
- Performance monitoring
- Worker behavior prediction

**SaaS Examples:**
- Applicant tracking systems (ATS) with AI-powered resume screening
- Performance management tools that score employees
- Workforce management software that assigns tasks
- Employee monitoring tools

## Category 5: Access to Essential Services

AI systems that evaluate eligibility for:
- Healthcare services
- Social benefits (welfare, housing, unemployment)
- Emergency services dispatch
- Credit and lending

**SaaS Example:** Credit scoring APIs, insurance risk assessment tools, healthcare triage systems.

## Category 6: Law Enforcement

AI systems used by law enforcement for:
- Risk assessment of individuals
- Lie detection
- Crime prediction (predictive policing)
- Evidence evaluation

**SaaS Example:** Not applicable to most SaaS—applies to government contracts for public safety software.

## Category 7: Migration, Asylum, and Border Control

AI systems used to:
- Assess security risks of travelers
- Process visa/asylum applications
- Verify travel documents

**SaaS Example:** Not applicable to most SaaS—applies to government immigration software.

## Category 8: Administration of Justice and Democratic Processes

AI systems that:
- Assist judicial decisions
- Influence legal proceedings
- Interpret facts or law

**SaaS Example:** Legal tech platforms that provide case outcome predictions or contract analysis (if used by courts).

## Article 6.3 Exceptions: When You're NOT High-Risk

Even if you fall into one of these categories, you may qualify for an exception under Article 6.3 if your AI system performs:
- A **narrow procedural task** (e.g., converting audio to text)
- **Pattern detection** (e.g., finding duplicates or anomalies)
- **Preparatory tasks** (e.g., data cleaning)

These exceptions are narrow and technical. Most SaaS products do NOT qualify.
