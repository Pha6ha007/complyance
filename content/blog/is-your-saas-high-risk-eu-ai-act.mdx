The EU AI Act introduces a risk-based classification system that determines your compliance obligations. If your SaaS product uses AI to make decisions about people—whether in hiring, credit scoring, content moderation, or personalized recommendations—you might be operating a high-risk AI system. Understanding this classification is crucial because high-risk systems face the strictest requirements, including mandatory risk assessments, technical documentation, and ongoing monitoring.

## What Makes an AI System "High-Risk"?

The EU AI Act defines high-risk AI systems in Annex III, which covers eight specific categories. These include AI systems used in employment, education, law enforcement, and credit scoring, among others. The key factor is whether your system makes or significantly influences decisions that affect people's access to opportunities, services, or rights.

## Common SaaS Use Cases That May Be High-Risk

Many popular SaaS features could trigger high-risk classification:

- **Recruitment tools** that screen resumes or rank candidates
- **Performance management** systems that evaluate employees
- **Credit assessment** features that determine loan eligibility
- **Content recommendation** systems that profile users
- **Fraud detection** tools that make automated blocking decisions

## What This Means for Your Business

If your SaaS is classified as high-risk, you'll need to implement comprehensive compliance measures before the August 2, 2026 deadline. This includes establishing a risk management system, maintaining detailed technical documentation, ensuring human oversight, and implementing post-market monitoring.
