Many SaaS companies believe that because they use OpenAI's API or Anthropic's Claude, they're not responsible for AI compliance—after all, they're not building the AI themselves. This is a dangerous misconception. Under the EU AI Act, if you **deploy** an AI system (even one built by a third party), you have compliance obligations. This guide explains your responsibilities and how to conduct vendor due diligence.

## Provider vs. Deployer: Who's Responsible?

The EU AI Act distinguishes between:

- **Provider**: The entity that develops and places an AI system on the market (e.g., OpenAI, Anthropic, Google)
- **Deployer**: The entity that uses the AI system under its authority (e.g., your SaaS company)

**Key point:** If you integrate OpenAI's API into your recruitment tool to screen candidates, **you are the deployer**—and you're responsible for ensuring the AI system complies with EU AI Act requirements for your specific use case.

## What Deployers Must Do

Deployers have distinct obligations under the EU AI Act, including:

1. **Use the AI system according to instructions** provided by the provider
2. **Monitor the AI system's operation** in your specific context
3. **Implement human oversight measures** (Article 14)
4. **Conduct a conformity assessment** if you modify the AI system significantly
5. **Report serious incidents** to regulatory authorities (Article 73)

## Vendor Due Diligence: Questions to Ask Your AI Provider

When evaluating AI vendors, ask:

### 1. Data Training & Privacy
- Is our data used to train your models?
- Where is our data processed (EU, US, other)?
- Do you have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)?

### 2. EU AI Act Compliance
- Do you provide model cards or technical documentation?
- Have you conducted a conformity assessment for high-risk use cases?
- Do you support EU AI Act compliance for deployers?

### 3. Risk Management
- What is your approach to bias testing and fairness?
- Do you have incident reporting procedures?
- Can we audit your model's performance?

## How Major AI Vendors Stack Up

### OpenAI
- **Data Training**: No training on API data (as of 2025 policy)
- **Location**: US-based, data processed globally
- **DPA**: Available for enterprise customers
- **Model Cards**: Limited public documentation
- **EU AI Act Support**: Still developing compliance tools

### Anthropic
- **Data Training**: No training on API data by default
- **Location**: US-based
- **DPA**: Available
- **Model Cards**: Strong model documentation
- **EU AI Act Support**: Constitutional AI approach aligns with transparency requirements

### AWS Bedrock / Google Vertex AI
- **Data Training**: Configurable—can opt out
- **Location**: EU regions available
- **DPA**: Comprehensive enterprise agreements
- **Model Cards**: Depends on underlying model provider
- **EU AI Act Support**: Enterprise-grade compliance support

## The Bottom Line: You're Still Responsible

Even if your AI vendor is fully compliant, **you must ensure the AI system is appropriate for your specific use case**. A model that's fine for content generation may not be suitable for high-risk employment decisions without additional safeguards.
