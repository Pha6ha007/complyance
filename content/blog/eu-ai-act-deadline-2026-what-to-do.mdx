With the EU AI Act's high-risk compliance deadline on August 2, 2026, SaaS companies have less than 6 months to implement comprehensive AI compliance measures. If you're selling into the EU market and your product uses AI for decision-making, classification, or profiling, this deadline applies to you. Here's what you need to do right now to avoid regulatory penalties and maintain market access.

## Understanding the August 2026 Deadline

The EU AI Act became law in August 2024, with a two-year transition period for high-risk AI systems. After August 2, 2026, any high-risk AI system operating in the EU must be fully compliant. Non-compliance can result in fines up to €15 million or 3% of global annual revenue—whichever is higher.

## Your 6-Month Compliance Roadmap

Here's a practical timeline for achieving compliance:

### Month 1-2: Classification & Gap Analysis
- Inventory all AI systems in your product
- Classify each system by risk level
- Identify which regulations apply (EU AI Act, Colorado AI Act, NYC LL144)
- Conduct gap analysis against requirements

### Month 3-4: Documentation & Risk Management
- Implement risk management system (Article 9)
- Create Annex IV technical documentation
- Establish data governance processes
- Document training data and model architecture

### Month 5-6: Human Oversight & Monitoring
- Implement human oversight measures (Article 14)
- Set up post-market monitoring system (Article 72)
- Create incident reporting procedures
- Conduct final compliance review

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Many SaaS companies make these mistakes:
- Assuming they're not covered because they're "just software"
- Waiting until the last minute to start compliance work
- Underestimating the documentation requirements
- Ignoring vendor due diligence (if you use third-party AI)
